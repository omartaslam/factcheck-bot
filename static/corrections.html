Corrections

Corrections Policy

We get things wrong. When that happens, we correct the record promptly and transparently. This page explains how to report an error, how we review it, and how corrections are published. Accuracy matters more than protecting a verdict.

Our Commitment

Fred is an AI-assisted fact-checking system. Despite our best efforts to eliminate bias and errors through the published Fred Charter and our methodology, verdicts can be wrong. Sources can be incomplete. The AI can make errors of reasoning or evidence weighting.

We commit to:

✓

Reviewing every correction request submitted in good faith

✓

Acknowledging receipt within 48 hours

✓

Completing a full editorial review within 5 working days

✓

Publishing corrections transparently — not silently editing verdicts

✓

Explaining clearly what was wrong and what the correct information is

What We Correct

Material factual errors — where evidence cited is wrong, misattributed, or misrepresented.

Source errors — where a source is described incorrectly or a key source was missed that would have materially changed the verdict.

Verdict miscalibration — where the verdict does not follow from the evidence according to the published methodology or charter rules.

Bias pattern violations — where a verdict applies one of the named violation patterns in the charter (e.g. conflict source hierarchy, religious double standard, good actor / bad actor framing).

We do not issue corrections for disagreements of interpretation where the evidence is genuinely contested, or for differences of opinion that do not involve factual error.

Review Process

Submission received

Your correction request is logged and assigned to a human editor. You receive an acknowledgement within 48 hours.

Editorial review

The editor reviews the original claim, the sources consulted, the verdict, and the evidence you have provided. The review is conducted against the methodology and charter — not against the original AI output.

Decision

We inform you of the outcome: correction upheld, partially upheld, or not upheld, with reasons. We aim to complete this within 5 working days of receipt.

Correction published

Where a correction is upheld, it is published alongside the original verdict with a clear note explaining what was wrong and what has changed. The original verdict text is retained for transparency — we do not silently rewrite history.

Escalation

If you disagree with our decision, you may request escalation to senior editorial review. We will respond within 10 working days.

IFCN complaints: If you believe Fred is in violation of the IFCN Code of Principles, you may submit a complaint directly to IFCN at ifcncodeofprinciples.poynter.org. We support this process and will cooperate fully with any IFCN-initiated review.
